Chunking and Embedding Space

Building with Claude — Retrieval-Grounded Responses

Why this matters: Before anything can be retrieved, a document has to become a set of small, addressable, embedded chunks. Get the chunk size or overlap wrong and a fact can become unretrievable no matter how good the vector store is. Everything below is fully offline and interactive — nothing sends a real request.

Why chunk overlap exists

    
The same SOP sentence, chunked two ways. Click Simulate on
      each to see whether the exception clause survives intact in at least one chunk.


    
Source text: "A credit score below the applicable minimum is
      grounds for automatic decline. If the credit bureau is temporarily unavailable, the
      application must be referred to the Credit Committee, not declined."


    

      

        
❌ chunk_size=20, overlap=0

        
chunk 0 (words 0-19):
  "...must be referred to the"

chunk 1 (words 20-39):
  "Credit Committee, not declined."

        
        

          🚨 Exception clause split across the boundary. A query about "what
          happens if the credit bureau is unavailable" retrieves chunk 0, which ends at "...must be
          referred to the" — the actual outcome ("Credit Committee, not declined") is in chunk 1,
          which the vector store may or may not also return. The answer is incomplete either way.
        

      


      

        
✅ chunk_size=20, overlap=8

        
chunk 0 (words 0-19):
  "...must be referred to the"

chunk 1 (words 12-31, 8-word overlap):
  "...referred to the Credit
   Committee, not declined."

        
        

          ✅ Exception clause intact in chunk 1. Even if the vector store only
          returns one of the two chunks, chunk 1's 8-word overlap window carries the full
          "referred to the Credit Committee, not declined" clause — the answer doesn't depend on
          getting both chunks back.
