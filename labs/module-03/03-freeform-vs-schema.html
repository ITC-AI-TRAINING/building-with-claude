Freeform vs. Schema

Building with Claude — why messages.parse() is safer than create() + json.loads()

Why this matters: Both approaches below "work" most of the time — which is exactly why the difference is easy to miss until a pipeline breaks in production. Click Simulate on each card a few times to see the shape of the difference. Fully offline — nothing sends a real request.

Simulate the same extraction, twice

Click Simulate repeatedly on each side — the naive approach's outcome changes depending on how Claude happens to format that response; the schema-driven side doesn't have that variable at all.

❌ create() + json.loads()
response = client.messages.create(...)
text = response.content[0].text
data = json.loads(text)
✅ messages.parse(output_format=...)
response = client.messages.parse(
    ..., output_format=LoanApplicationRecord)
record = response.parsed_output

What schema-driven parsing does and doesn't guarantee

Don't over-claim what messages.parse() buys you — it guarantees shape, not truth.

ClaimGuaranteed by messages.parse()?
Output is valid JSON matching the schema's typesYes
loan_type is one of the 4 declared Literal valuesYes
loan_amount_inr is a float greater than 0Yes — enforced by the field_validator
No raw-text parsing step to break on a fence or stray proseYes
The extracted values are factually correct / not hallucinatedNo — that's Module 7's job
The source text actually contained enough information to answerNo — see Demo 2's unrecoverable case
