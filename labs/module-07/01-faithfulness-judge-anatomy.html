Faithfulness Judge Anatomy

Building with Claude — Evaluation and Output Quality

Why this matters: A retrieval check (Module 6) only asks whether something relevant was found — it can't tell you whether the generated answer actually says what its cited chunk supports. Faithfulness needs a second Claude call that reads both and judges whether they agree. Everything below is fully offline — nothing sends a real request.

Plausible is not the same as faithful

Both answers below cite [policy-0] and sound equally confident. Click Judge this answer on each to see what the faithfulness judge actually catches.

Context [policy-0]: "Maximum loan-to-value ratio for home loans is 90% up to INR 30 lakhs, 80% between 30 and 75 lakhs, and 75% above 75 lakhs."
Question: "What is the maximum loan-to-value ratio for home loans?"
✅ Faithful answer
"The maximum LTV for home
  loans is 90% up to INR 30
  lakhs, 80% between 30 and 75
  lakhs, and 75% above 75 lakhs
  [policy-0]."
✅ score: 1 — every number in the answer traces directly to the cited chunk's text; nothing invented.
❌ Hallucinated answer
"The maximum LTV for home
  loans is 95%, regardless of
  loan amount [policy-0]."
🚨 score: 0 — unsupported_claims: ["95% regardless of loan amount"] — the cited chunk gives three tiered percentages, none of them 95%. The citation tag is present and correctly formatted; the number behind it is simply not there. A relevance-only check would have passed this answer.
